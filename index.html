Cost to Cover Reversal Risk

Coverage assumption — two very different targets. The Permanence Trust is explicitly designed to grow toward 100% replacement of all 250,000 tCO₂ if the project fully reverses. The buffer pool withholds only 10–20% of credits and can cover only that withheld fraction — the remaining 80–90% of sold credits have no financial backstop if they reverse. The models are not symmetric: one is a partial reserve, the other targets full liability coverage.

The DACCS Signal
Spend a small share of the PT premium on DACCS today. The tonnage will look negligible — that's expected at current prices. But the act of buying matters: it signals real, creditworthy demand, contributes to the commercial viability of early DACCS projects, and helps push the cost curve down for everyone. Meanwhile, the remaining premium still compounds toward the break-even target — the hedge costs you very little in coverage terms.
